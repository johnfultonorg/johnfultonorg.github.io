Courses Taught

This page lists current and recent courses I have taught at Franklin University, including courses in computing, cybersecurity, network security, web development, analytics, IT strategy, marketing analytics, and career-focused experiential learning.

Current through Summer 2026

BUSA603

Terms: 25FA, 26SP

Course: Marketing Management & Analytics

Description: This course covers the application of analytics tools, techniques, strategies and methods to marketing management. Students learn to analyze market data, enabling management decisions to be based on data-driven facts and customer insights. Using marketing analytics tools to model scenarios, students learn how organizations can measure returns on investment relative to their marketing efforts, drive performance and strengthen the effectiveness of its campaigns.

COMP201

Terms: 24SU

Course: Computer Organization

Description: This course is the first of four courses that holistically explore the structure of computational systems. This course deals with the nature of computer hardware. The course will cover the structure of current computer systems at the level of functional organization, representation of data and programs, the design of the memory hierarchy, and, the design of the I/O system. The course will introduce basic assembly language.

COMP204

Terms: 24SU

Course: Computer Networks

Description: This course serves as an introduction to the function, design, administration, and implementation of computer networks. Topics include network infrastructure, architecture, protocols, applications, and the OSI networking model. Note, this course has proctored exam(s).

COMP691

Terms/Sections: 24FA, 25SP, 25SU, 25FA, 26SP, 26SU

Course: Capstone

Description: This course, the final one in the Master of Science - Computer Science program, challenges students to research a current topic of interest in Computer Science and produce an original paper and presentation on the topic. In addition to the research paper, students are introduced to the economics of software development and the tools needed to estimate the cost of a software development project for management in a corporate environment. The last topic in the course is a discussion of ethics as it relates to Information Technology. Current topics in ethics will be discussed through the use of relevant case studies. The recitation portion of this course focuses on job preparation and career development.

CYSC250 (was ISEC250)

Terms: 24SU, 25SU

Course: Cybersecurity for the Professions

Description: This course is a breadth-based cybersecurity course for non-technical majors. It aims to equip a bachelor’s degree seeker with no prior cybersecurity knowledge, other than what the newspaper reports say, with essential cybersecurity knowledge. The course introduces organizational, people, and technological aspects of cybersecurity. More specifically, it covers (1) governance, standards, and risk management topics in the organizational domain, (2) security awareness, privacy, ethics and cyber threats in the people domain, and (3) cyberspace, critical sectors, and emerging topics in the technological domain.

CYSC325

Terms/Sections: 26SU

Course: Network Security

Description: Networks are a major point of entry to most computer systems, making the prevention of unwanted intrusion, abuse, and flooding of communication channels an important organizational priority. This course focuses on preserving appropriate use of network resources while preventing disallowed use. Students learn how to employ firewalls, VPNs, and stateful packet inspection techniques to harden computer networks.

CYSC610

Terms: 24FA

Course: Information Assurance

Description: This course covers the fundamentals of security in the enterprise environment. Included are coverage of risks and vulnerabilities, threat modeling, policy formation, controls and protection methods, encryption and authentication technologies, network security, cryptography, personnel and physical security issues, as well as ethical and legal issues. This foundational course serves as an introduction to many of the subsequent topics discussed in depth in later security courses. Note, this course has proctored exam(s). This exam may require additional technology if the student uses online proctoring.

ITEC136

Terms/Sections: 24SU, 26SU

Course: Principles of Programming

Description: This course introduces programming to individuals with little or no programming background. The goal of this course is to introduce the fundamentals of structured programming, problem solving, algorithm design, and software lifecycle. Topics will include testing, data types, operations, repetition and selection control structures, functions and procedures, arrays, and top down stepwise refinement. Students will design, code, test, debug, and document programs in a relevant programming language.

ITEC660

Terms/Sections: 24FA, 25SP, 25SU, 25FA, 26SP, 26SU

Course: Web Development and Deployment

Description: This course builds web applications by combining software development, database, and cloud concepts into a modern web development course. Students will use current technologies in all three areas to design, develop, and deploy web applications in cloud-based environments. Topics will include web frameworks, model-view-controller (MVC) or model-view-viewmodel (MVVM) architectures, front- and back-end technologies, asynchronous web requests, database integration, security, and cloud deployment design decisions.

ITEC690

Terms/Sections: 25SP, 25FA, 26SP, 26SU

Course: IT Strategy and Policy

Description: This course focuses on the value of Information Technology within an organization. For many organizations, IT is a cost; for others, it is a strategic advantage. Emphasis is placed on organizational efficiency and leadership of IT organizations to enhance business value and organizational performance. The recitation portion of this course focuses on job preparation and career development.

MATH601

Terms: 24FA, 25SP, 25FA, 26SP

Course: Intro to Analytics

Description: This course provides an introductory overview of methods, concepts, and current practices in the growing field of statistics and data analytics. Topics to be covered include data collection, data analysis and visualization as well as probability, statistical inference and regression methods for informed decision-making. Students will explore these topics with current statistical software. Some emphasis will also be given to ethical principles of data analytics.

PF511

Terms: 26SP

Course: Experiential Tech Learning Lab

Description: This 12-week, 1-credit, repeatable course provides students with hands-on opportunities to engage in career-oriented co-curricular activities and improve essential skills for success in the technology industry. Students will participate in real-world learning experiences such as industry events, competitions, or technical projects while mastering career development strategies. Topics include market research, application materials, networking, interviewing, and navigating early career challenges. Through reflective assignments and career-focused activities, students will build both technical and soft skills critical to career success.

WEBD445

Terms: 25SP

Course: Advanced Web Development

Description: This course will look at the state of technology in web development. It will cover topics that are cutting edge and new as well as those that may not get significant treatment in other courses.
