Top 4 Ideas

1. Use Strong, Unique Passwords and Enable MFA

  • Use long passphrases (e.g., “Purple$River!Morning2025”).
  • Never reuse passwords across accounts.
  • Turn on multi-factor authentication (MFA) wherever possible — especially for email, banking, and cloud services.

2. Keep Systems and Software Updated

  • Enable automatic updates on all devices and applications.
  • Patch routers, smart devices, and antivirus tools regularly.
  • Outdated software is one of the most common points of entry for attackers.

3. Be Alert to Phishing and Social Engineering

  • Pause before clicking links or opening attachments in unexpected messages.
  • Verify senders independently (e.g., call or use a known address).
  • Train employees and family members to spot urgent, suspicious, or emotional language.

4. Back Up and Protect Your Data

  • Use automated, encrypted backups to both local and cloud storage.
  • Test recovery periodically.
  • Store sensitive files only on secure, access-controlled systems — not on shared drives or USB sticks.
